population modeling with the logistic curve model

given dy/dt = ky(1-y/M), the logistics differential equation, general solution is y = M/(1 + be^(-Mkt)), where M is the carrying capacity, b is a constant that controls where the curve crosses the y axis, and a = M/(1+b), or the initial value/y-intercept.this can be applied to many contexts, especially in such as carrying capacity and initial population. given the variables, it is possible to solve for and graph this curve, which is what this website does. (NOTE: "past population" and "current population" refer to the less recent and more recent populations, respectively, not necessarily one from the past and the current one.)
