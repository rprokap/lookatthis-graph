population modeling with the logistic curve model

given dy/dt = ky(1-y/M), the logistics differential equation, general solution is y = M/(1 + be^(-Mkt)), where M is the carrying capacity, b is a constant that controls where the curve crosses the y axis, and a = M/(1+b), or the initial value/y-intercept.this can be applied to many contexts, especially in such as carrying capacity and initial population. this program graphs the approximate population growth of the human population on earth according to the logistic curve approximation.


In an instance with limited resources such as this one, the differential equation dy/dt = ky(M-y) is used. y_0 is the initial population, y is the population after t years, M is the carrying capacity, and k is a specific constant utilized in modeling the growth rate. The solution of this differential equation is y = (y_0*M)/(y_0 + (M-y_0)*e^(-k*M*t)). With the data that the earth's human population was approximately 7.550 billion on July 1, 2017 and 9.633 billion on July 1, 2018, and that the carrying capacity is estimated to be approximately 9.5 billion, the k constant can be solved. With this, the population approximations for various years can be determined.
